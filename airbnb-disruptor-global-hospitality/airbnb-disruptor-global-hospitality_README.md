# Airbnb: A Disruptor in Global Hospitality

## Case
Airbnb has grown into a global hospitality platform with over 8 million listings, 5+ million hosts, and $11.1 billion in 2024 revenue. The case asks: as a senior Airbnb executive, how would you use data to help the company evolve from a lodging platform into a full service travel ecosystem?

## Objective
Discuss how data can enable that transition, identify the specific data components needed, assess how Airbnb's existing dataset could be used toward that goal, and identify what additional data would be required beyond it.

## Approach
Mapped fields from Airbnb's existing listings dataset (neighborhood group, room type, price, minimum nights, availability, reviews per month, host listing counts) to the ecosystem insights they support, then identified the categories of data Airbnb does not currently have but would need to bundle stays with transport, experiences, and other travel services.

## Key Findings
- Airbnb's existing dataset is strong for identifying geographic demand patterns, pricing levels, and stay duration, which support bundling decisions and inventory forecasting.
- The platform's data is largely transactional (price, availability) and does not capture customer motivations, trip purpose, or spending outside of the platform, which limits its ability to design true stay-plus-transport-plus-experience bundles.
- Regulatory complexity (registration, occupancy limits, taxation) and host trust and safety issues are real constraints on scaling beyond lodging.
- Competing in the broader travel ecosystem means competing against players with deep expertise in adjacent categories (flights, car rental, local experiences).

## Recommendation
Pursue the ecosystem strategy through partnerships with tour operators, mobility providers, and local hosts rather than building every capability in-house, paired with stronger vetting and compliance processes. In parallel, expand data collection to include trip purpose, companion type, spending categories, and transport and experience preferences (via surveys, in-app prompts, or partnerships with airlines, ride-share firms, and event organizers), then apply predictive modeling to personalize travel bundles, backed by clear data governance to maintain regulatory compliance and guest trust.
